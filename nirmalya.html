Project Nirmalya

“The earth will not continue to offer its harvest, except with faithful stewardship. We cannot say we love the land and then take steps to destroy it for use by future generations.” ― Pope John Paul II

Why we do it

The menace of waste accumulation is plaguing Delhi. If you've ever been to the capital its quite obvious that the numerous landfills, piled with millions of tons of waste would have attracted your eyeballs. A staggering 9000 metric tons of waste is dumped in the landfills on a daily basis. Understanding the severity of the situation, we decided to take the onus upon ourselves and endeavour to eradicate this problem from the Heart of India.

What we do

We've adopted a method of decentralisation to tackle this problem. In this approach we intend to create Zero-Waste Societies by ensuring segregation ,either at source or via means of manual intervention into wet and dry components, using the wet waste for composting and dry waste for recycling, empowering waste collectors/gardeners in the process. We are also designing novel user centric designs of home and community composters.

How we do it

We execute our plan in different steps. Our first step is conducting awareness sessions in the societies, educating the residents about the perils of this menace and the techniques of waste segregation. In our community composting model, We channelise this wet waste into the common compost plants(mesh or pit), located nearby the society. In houses with home composters, the residents themselves channel the waste to small composters within their homes.

Social Impact

Our model is creating a more aware society of residents who realise their responsibility of adapting sustainable consumption habits.The gardeners/waste collectors are also being provided a means of creating meaningful impact, thereby raising their digniy of work . In future, we plan rigorous awareness sessions targeted at younger generation.

Environmental Impact

The community composting model catering to horticulture waste of one society, is expected to prevent apprixmately 5000kg of waste per month from going into landfills and reduce 14 Kg of Green House Gas Emmissions annually.. There will also be savings on pollution caused by transportation of waste via trucks.

Economical Impact

The composted produced will be utilised in house allowing societies to save on compost pruchase.The waste collectors/gardeners are also being financially empowered by direct hikes in their salary as incentive for management of the composting set up.The surplus compost produced can also be sold to nurseries, depending on the contract with the societies.
